Six Sigma

Focus: Six Sigma focuses on reducing defects and variation in processes.

Goal: The goal of Six Sigma is to achieve near-perfect quality (3.4 defects per million opportunities or less).

Methodology: It employs a structured, data-driven approach (DMAIC - Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control) to identify and eliminate causes of defects and variation.

Tools: Six Sigma utilizes statistical tools and techniques to measure performance, analyze root causes of problems, and improve processes systematically.

Lean

Focus: Lean focuses on eliminating waste (non-value-added activities) and improving flow in processes.

Goal: The goal of Lean is to maximize customer value while minimizing waste.

Methodology: It emphasizes continuous improvement (Kaizen), respect for people, and value stream mapping to identify and eliminate waste.

Tools: Lean tools include techniques like 5S (Sort, Set in order, Shine, Standardize, Sustain), Kanban (pull system), and Just-In-Time (JIT) production to streamline processes and improve efficiency.

Six Sigma Lean (or Lean Six Sigma)

Integration: Lean Six Sigma combines the principles of both Six Sigma and Lean to create a comprehensive approach to process improvement.

Benefits: It leverages the strengths of each methodology - Six Sigma's focus on quality and reduction of defects, and Lean's emphasis on waste reduction and process flow improvement.

Application: Organizations use Lean Six Sigma to achieve operational excellence, improve customer satisfaction, reduce costs, and enhance overall business performance.
